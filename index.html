About the Celebration

A Momentous Anniversary for the Hula People

In 2026, the Hula community of Central Province, Papua New Guinea, gathers to mark a milestone that has shaped the very soul of its people — 150 years since the Gospel first arrived on their shores. What began with the arrival of a lone missionary in 1876 blossomed into a living faith that has sustained generations, built families, shaped education, and knit together a proud coastal community.

The anniversary celebrations are scheduled for 23rd–28th June 2026, bringing together Hula families from across Papua New Guinea and the diaspora in a week of thanksgiving, worship, cultural heritage, and fellowship. These events honour the faithfulness of God, the courage of early missionaries, and the enduring spirit of the Hula people.

To support the celebration, a Corporate Dinner Fundraiser is being held on Saturday 2nd May 2026 in Port Moresby. Your generous support helps make this historic commemoration possible.

Our Heritage

George Lawes & the Gospel in Hula

In 1876, a missionary from the London Missionary Society (LMS) by the name of George Lawes stepped ashore at Hula village on the southern coast of what is now Papua New Guinea's Central Province. His arrival would mark one of the most consequential moments in the history of the Hula people.

"The Gospel came to Hula not by force, but by love — and it has stayed by faith."

Lawes promised the Hula that he would send teachers, men from the Cook Islands to come and teach them the gospel of Christ, learning their language, building trust, and sharing the message of the Gospel with patience and compassion. The Hula people, celebrated throughout the region for their seafaring mastery and fishing skills, received this message with open hearts and willing minds.

Over the decades, the Christian faith became woven into the very fabric of Hula identity — shaping how the community celebrated life's milestones, raised its children, and stood together through hardship. Churches rose, schools were established, and generation upon generation of Hula Christians went on to serve as pastors, teachers, nurses, and leaders throughout Papua New Guinea.

Today, as descendants of those first converts gather to celebrate 150 years, they do so as a living testimony to God's faithfulness — a heritage to be celebrated, preserved, and passed on to the generations still to come.
